Project Overview

A comprehensive Excel-based Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model designed for business valuation. This model incorporates multiple scenarios, sensitivity analysis, and detailed financial projections to determine intrinsic business value.

Model Architecture

The valuation model is structured across multiple interconnected worksheets:

  • Assumptions: Key inputs including growth rates, margins, and discount rates
  • Historical Financials: 5-year historical performance data
  • Projections: 10-year forward-looking financial forecasts
  • Free Cash Flow: Calculation of unlevered free cash flow
  • WACC Calculation: Weighted average cost of capital determination
  • Valuation: DCF calculation and terminal value
  • Sensitivity Analysis: Multiple scenario testing
  • Output Dashboard: Visual summary of valuation results

Key Components

Revenue Projections

Multi-driver revenue model with segment-level forecasting

WACC Calculation

Dynamic cost of capital incorporating market data

Scenario Analysis

Base, optimistic, and pessimistic case valuations

Sensitivity Tables

Two-way data tables for key assumptions

Valuation Methodology

The model employs industry-standard DCF methodology:

  1. Project Free Cash Flows: Forecast operating cash flows for explicit period (10 years)
  2. Calculate WACC: Determine appropriate discount rate based on capital structure and risk
  3. Discount Cash Flows: Apply WACC to bring future cash flows to present value
  4. Terminal Value: Calculate perpetuity value using Gordon Growth Model
  5. Enterprise Value: Sum of PV of cash flows and terminal value
  6. Equity Value: Adjust for net debt to arrive at equity value per share

Scenario Analysis Results

Three scenarios were modeled to capture range of potential outcomes:

Base Case Scenario

Assumptions: 5% revenue growth, 18% EBITDA margin, 8.5% WACC

Valuation: $450M enterprise value ($32 per share)

Optimistic Scenario

Assumptions: 8% revenue growth, 20% EBITDA margin, 8.0% WACC

Valuation: $580M enterprise value ($41 per share)

Pessimistic Scenario

Assumptions: 2% revenue growth, 15% EBITDA margin, 9.5% WACC

Valuation: $320M enterprise value ($23 per share)

Sensitivity Analysis

The model includes comprehensive sensitivity testing for key value drivers:

  • WACC vs. Terminal Growth: Tests valuation sensitivity to discount rate and perpetual growth assumptions
  • Revenue Growth vs. EBITDA Margin: Analyzes impact of top-line growth and profitability
  • CapEx vs. Working Capital: Evaluates effect of investment requirements on free cash flow

Key finding: Valuation is most sensitive to WACC assumptions, with a 1% change in WACC resulting in approximately 15% change in enterprise value.

Model Features

  • Conditional formatting for easy identification of key metrics
  • Charts and graphs for visual representation of projections
  • Comprehensive documentation of formulas and assumptions

Applications

This valuation model can be applied to various business scenarios:

  • Mergers and acquisitions due diligence
  • Investment decision-making
  • Strategic planning and target setting
  • Fairness opinions and expert testimony
  • Private equity and venture capital investments

Conclusion

This DCF model demonstrates proficiency in financial modeling, valuation theory, and Excel functionality. The comprehensive approach to scenario and sensitivity analysis provides stakeholders with a robust framework for understanding business value under various conditions.
